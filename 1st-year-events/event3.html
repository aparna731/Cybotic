Workshop on “Blockchain and Dapps Development For Cybersecurity”

28/7/23

Speakers Profile :
Narendra V. G is currently pursuing a full-time PhD in the Department of CSE at MIT, Manipal, a journey he embarked on in 2017. With twenty-five years of experience in the Department of CSE, he possesses expertise in developing and testing publications such as 'A trusted IoT data sharing and secure oracle-based access for agricultural production risk management.' His portfolio includes twenty-seven journal publications and thirty-three conference presentations. Narendra is dedicated to fostering an engaged research and learning environment. He also delivers technical talks on Computer Vision and Applications, such as his presentation at STJIT Ranebennur on February 2nd, 2012.
Namrata, a professor at MIT, Manipal, brings extensive experience to her role. She contributes to journals, including "A Conceptual BIoT Framework for Theft Protection and Marketing of Harvested Coconuts in Local Farms," and participates in conferences like the "Interdisciplinary Conference on Healthcare and Technical Research 2021" held from November 11th to 13th, 2021, at MAHE, Manipal, INDIA. Her work extends to "Blockchain Framework for Agro Financing of Farmers in South India" and the 5th International Conference on "Emerging Technologies in Computer Engineering: Cognitive Computing and Intelligent IoT."
Mr. Manoj T is currently pursuing a full-time PhD in the Department of CSE at MIT, Manipal, starting from October 2020. With six years of teaching experience in the Department of CSE and two years of industry experience at Philips India Ltd, he brings a wealth of knowledge to his research. His portfolio includes four international conference presentations and six journal research publications. Like his peers, Manoj is dedicated to promoting an engaged research and learning environment. Dr. Shivananda V. Seeri, the Head of CSE (IoT & Cybersecurity with Blockchain Technology), warmly welcomed our guests.

Mr. Manoj T addressed the gathering, posing questions such as "What is IoT and CyberSecurity?" and "Dapps Development for Cybersecurity."
The speaker proceeded to elaborate on IoT, defining it as a system of interrelated computing devices, mechanical and digital machines, objects, animals, or people equipped with unique identifiers (UIDs) capable of transferring data over a network without necessitating human-to-human or human-to-computer interaction. He also delved into the distinction between IoT and IoT devices, exploring the core meaning of cybersecurity and its benefits, as well as how it's developed with the assistance of blockchain technology.
During his talk, Mr. Manoj T specifically covered topics such as the shift from centralized to decentralized systems, the fundamentals of blockchain and smart contracts, dispelling myths about blockchain, discussing generations of blockchain technology, layered architecture for blockchain, types of blockchains, and the relevance of blockchain in the context of cybersecurity, particularly within the realm of IoT. He also highlighted popular frameworks, tools, and programming languages for blockchain development and guided students on where to find learning resources to enhance their understanding of IoT and cybersecurity.

In conclusion, Mr. Manoj T emphasized the significance of understanding IoT, its design, and management, along with providing a brief overview of cybersecurity. To ensure clarity, a quick Q&A session was held at the end of the session, addressing any doubts and queries. This informative session was attended by approximately 66 second-year students specializing in CSE IoT & Cybersecurity with Blockchain Technology, enriching their knowledge in the fields of blockchain and Dapps Development for Cybersecurity.
